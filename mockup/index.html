CO2 emissions from transport (% of total fuel combustion)

CO2 emissions from transport contains emissions from the combustion of fuel for all transport activity, regardless of the sector, except for international marine bunkers and international aviation. This includes domestic aviation, domestic navigation, road, rail and pipeline transport, and corresponds to IPCC Source/Sink Category 1 A 3.

CO2 emissions from electricity and heat production, total (% of total fuel combustion)

CO2 emissions from electricity and heat production is the sum of three IEA categories of CO2 emissions: (1) Main Activity Producer Electricity and Heat which contains the sum of emissions from main activity producer electricity generation, combined heat and power generation and heat plants. Main activity producers (formerly known as public utilities) are defined as those undertakings whose primary activity is to supply the public. They may be publicly or privately owned. This corresponds to IPCC Source/Sink Category 1 A 1 a. For the CO2 emissions from fuel combustion (summary) file, emissions from own on-site use of fuel in power plants (EPOWERPLT) are also included. (2) Unallocated Autoproducers which contains the emissions from the generation of electricity and/or heat by autoproducers. Autoproducers are defined as undertakings that generate electricity and/or heat, wholly or partly for their own use as an activity which supports their primary activity. They may be privately or publicly owned. In the 1996 IPCC Guidelines, these emissions would normally be distributed between industry, transport and "other" sectors. (3) Other Energy Industries contains emissions from fuel combusted in petroleum refineries, for the manufacture of solid fuels, coal mining, oil and gas extraction and other energy-producing industries.

Beginners' guide

Tabs

Website is divided into 2 tabs:

Map & details Timespan

First tab shows data for 1 year. Second tab may display for complete timespan.

To see details for 1 year and complete timespan chart list of countries must be defined.

Countries list

To define country user must click from list located in sidebar on left side of map. Next to country name is displayed value for particular map type. Background color depends on value displayed on map.

  • Greenlandno data

List may be hidden if hide arrow button has been clicked. To show list, click on arrow on blue background in the corner of the map.

Once countries are defined processing must be triggered with Processing button. Button is disabled until any country is not selected. It may last up to 2 minutes. Over 1 Megabyte of data must be processed.

Before processing year may be change and radar charts will display for this year. Processing must be repeated after year change.

Map

Map is being coloured depending on value for each country and selected data type. Data types might be changed with button below the legend:

Data is displayed into 11 categories and each has own color, depending on map data category. Each data category has same color for missing data for particular country.

Radar

Radar charts are displaying detailed data from 1 year for selected countries. Data must be processed for each year.

It has been noted that after changing list of country radar charts are not displayed properly. Please refresh website to omit this issue.

Timespan

Timespan consists of list of available charts on the top. List is same as for radar charts, displayed in details for 1 year.

After defining countries and processing go to tab Timespan. Select one of categories to see a stacked chart. Chart timespan may be narrowed using interactive select above the chart.

Explanation
Global greenhouse gas emissions are broken down by sectoral sources in the sections which follow:

Transport: domestic aviation, road transportation, rail transportation, domestic navigation, other transportation.

Energy (energy, manufacturing and construction industries and fugitive emissions): emissions are inclusive of public heat and electricity production; other energy industries; fugitive emissions from solid fuels, oil and gas, manufacturing industries and construction.

International bunkers: international aviation; international navigation/shipping.

Residential, commercial, institutional and AFF: Residential and other sectors.

Industry (industrial processes and product use): production of minerals, chemicals, metals, pulp/paper/food/drink, halocarbons, refrigeration and air conditioning; aerosols and solvents; semicondutor/electronics manufacture; electrical equipment.

Waste: solid waste disposal; wastewater handling; waste incineration; other waste handling.

Land use: emissions from the net conversion of forest; cropland; grassland and burning biomass for agriculture and other uses, including Argiculture category :methane and nitrous oxide emissions from enteric fermentation; manure management; rice cultivation; synthetic fertilizers; manure applied to soils; manure left on pasture; crop residues; burning crop residues, savanna and cultivation of organic soils.

Other sources: fossil fuel fires; indirect nitrous oxide from non-agricultural NOx and ammonia; other anthropogenic sources.
